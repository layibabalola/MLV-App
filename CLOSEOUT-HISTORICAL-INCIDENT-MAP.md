## Brief Answers

1. Which historical scenarios were impossible or unsafe to solve with scripting alone?

- Ambiguous historical branch and backup-branch pruning: scripts can prove ancestry, patch-id, and tree equality, but adjudication is needed to decide historical value and whether recovery evidence is sufficient.
- Merge-conflict remediation: scripts can create conflict packets and enforce scope, but agents/humans decide the semantic resolution.
- Dirty detached worktree cleanup: scripts can preserve bytes, but adjudication is needed when paths are sensitive, unowned, or ambiguous.
- Protected/stale locked worktrees: scripts can inspect locks and process hints, but active ownership and protected-root cleanup need judgment.
- Broad sweep mutation: scripts can enumerate candidates, but deciding whether bulk mutation is appropriate requires explicit review and audit.

2. Which scenarios required Codex/Claude/subagent/human judgment?

- Merge-required historical branches, backup/redundancy decisions, conflict remediation packets, dirty detached preservation, protected locked worktree cleanup, remediation freeze removal, and any broad-mutation override.
- MLV-App supports deterministic reviewers for exact tuple and policy checks, but ambiguous intent should be handled by a declared review surface such as `codex-primary-agent`, `codex-subagent`, `claude-subagent`, or `human-reviewer`.
